The League officially anounced the theme for Leg 1

The inspiration behind our theme, Power, Progress & Ethics of Control, comes from the urgent need to address transformative challenges in Botswana—and beyond—by harnessing our collective ability to enact change while remaining ethically grounded. Here’s how each element of the theme connects to the issues we're tackling in OLC Leg 1:

1. Power
- Empowerment Through Education:
The crisis in our education system—where thousands of students face limited opportunities due to poor exam results—illustrates a loss of personal and collective power. When students aren't adequately prepared, they lose the ability to influence their futures and contribute meaningfully to society. By engaging students and policymakers in actionable debates, we aim to restore this power, equipping young people with the skills and confidence needed to shape their destinies. It goes without saying, that the system is not tailored for unique learning needs of scholars, ergo, that is why we have partnered with GreyEd to foster personalized AI tutoring and learning that best fits every learner, no matter the background.

- Health and Social Advocacy:
In the realm of HIV and health advocacy, power also means controlling the narrative. Many still believe HIV isn't a major issue, which undermines public health efforts. Despite Botswana’s significant progress in HIV management, 20.8% of adults (15-65 years) remain affected, with 329,000 Batswana living with HIV. By fostering open discussions and informed debate, we empower communities with the knowledge to confront health challenges head-on.

2. Progress
- Driving Innovation in Education:
The partnership with companies like GreyEd to introduce personalized AI tutoring is a prime example of progress. It shows how modern technology can transform traditional education, making learning more accessible and tailored to individual needs—thus bridging gaps and ensuring students are better prepared for the future.

- Helping Communities:
It is one of our principles of virtue to give back to communities and help them strive in the efforts of improving and diversifying the economy. That is why we have partnered with Botswana Beacon of Hope Foundation to give back to marginalized communities in efforts to exterminate World Hunger and ensure national food security.
We have also partnered with Creatives Oddysey Cohort in our strides to diversify and promote the cultural and artistry sector of Botswana, by connecting different creatives with one another in order to elevate their work in their respective domains.

- Responding to Climate Change:
The recent floods in Gaborone highlight the tangible impacts of climate change. Addressing environmental challenges requires not only immediate action but also long-term progress in how we plan and adapt our infrastructures and policies to safeguard lives and property. In efforts to ignite policy discussions, we have adapted our debate motions, public speaking topics & poetry topics to catalyze progress in the climate change arena.

- Embracing Technological Advancements:
The rapid rise of AI is reshaping industries and labor markets globally. Preparing our youth to navigate this evolving landscape ensures that Botswana remains competitive in the digital economy, driving forward national progress while encouraging innovation.

3. Ethics of Control
- Balancing Innovation with Responsibility:
With great power and rapid progress comes the need for ethical control. In education, while AI has the potential to revolutionize learning, it’s crucial to manage its implementation in a way that safeguards fairness, privacy, and accessibility. And it goes without saying that in as much as it brings its good benefits, it also brings with it, cons.The same applies to the deployment of new technologies in various sectors. Those are the sorts of initiative we strive to shed light on to all communities.

- Regulating Environmental and Health Policies:
Whether it's controlling the factors that contribute to climate change or managing public health crises like HIV, there is an ethical imperative to design policies that protect the public interest without overstepping individual rights. Our platform encourages a dialogue where ethical considerations are as important as innovative solutions.

- Ensuring Fair Governance:
Across all these issues, ethical control means making decisions that are transparent, inclusive, and just. It’s about harnessing power to drive progress while ensuring that control mechanisms don’t become tools of oppression or inequality.

Connecting the Dots
By focusing on Power, Progress & Ethics of Control, OLC Leg 1 is not just a competition—it's a call to action:

- Power is about reclaiming and redistributing the ability to influence our future, whether through education, health advocacy, or technology.

- Progress represents the forward momentum needed to overcome current challenges and innovate for a better tomorrow.

- Ethics of Control ensures that as we progress and gain power, we do so responsibly, keeping the well-being of individuals and communities at the forefront.

This theme serves as both a lens to examine Botswana’s pressing issues and a framework for developing solutions that are innovative, equitable, and sustainable. It’s an invitation for every participant to consider not only how we can change the system but also how we can do it in a way that respects our shared values and promotes long-term societal well-being.

From Oratory League to Botswana; More Pressure, More Precious! That is how diamonds are made.
