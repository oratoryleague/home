Code of Conduct

Guiding Principles for Respectful Discourse & Professional Excellence

Our Commitment to Ethical Oratory

The Oratory League (OL) Code of Conduct establishes the ethical framework governing all interactions, events, and activities within our global community. This living document reflects our unwavering commitment to fostering an inclusive environment that celebrates diverse perspectives while maintaining the highest standards of professional conduct.

I. Core Principles

Integrity in Communication

All members shall maintain truthfulness, accuracy, and authenticity in speech and debate, properly attributing sources and avoiding deliberate misinformation.

Respect for Diversity

We champion respectful engagement across all cultural, ideological, and personal differences, prohibiting discrimination based on race, gender, religion, nationality, or orientation.

Professional Decorum

Members must maintain appropriate conduct during events, including proper decorum in speech, dress, and digital interactions.

II. Expected Behaviors

All OL members and participants agree to:

  • Maintain constructive dialogue that elevates discourse quality
  • Respect time limits and procedural rules during formal events
  • Protect confidential information shared through OL activities
  • Acknowledge and correct errors in factual claims promptly
  • Support fellow members' growth through constructive feedback

III. Prohibited Conduct

Strictly Forbidden Actions Include:

  • Plagiarism or intellectual property violations
  • Personal attacks or ad hominem arguments
  • Hate speech or discriminatory language
  • Sexual harassment or inappropriate physical contact
  • Deliberate disruption of events or activities
  • Unauthorized commercial solicitation

IV. Enforcement & Reporting

Reporting Procedures

Submit detailed incident reports within 14 days to ethics@oratoryleague.org. Anonymous reporting available for sensitive cases.

Investigation Process

All claims investigated by Ethics Committee within 30 days. Participants required to cooperate fully with investigations.

Disciplinary Actions

Ranging from warning to permanent expulsion, depending on severity. Appeals process available through Board Review.

V. Supplemental Policies

Digital Conduct

Extends to all online platforms, including social media interactions, virtual events, and OL-managed digital spaces.

Third-Party Events

OL standards apply to any event using our branding or organized by official chapters, regardless of location.

Policy Updates

Revised annually with member feedback. Current version effective January 1, 2025. Archived versions available upon request.
