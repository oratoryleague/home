Our History

From bold beginnings to revolutionary strides

A Journey of Resilience & Innovation

Founded in November 2024 by two visionary young orators, Asante Aupa Modala and Leatile King Baaitse – former scholars of Mater Spei College – Oratory League began as a bold initiative. Faced with financial constraints that barred them from participating in the 2024 Orate Africa Championships under school sponsorship, these determined leaders forged a new path. With unwavering resolve, they secured independent participation, ignited community support, and, through relentless negotiations in Francistown, rallied sponsors to register a team of 11 talented delegates.

From its humble beginnings as a coalition of independent debaters and public speakers, Oratory League rapidly evolved into a revolutionary company. Today, it not only leads in oratory education but also makes significant strides in establishing an orate judiciary that champions structured debate and intellectual discourse.

Milestones

The Bold Beginning

In November 2024, amid financial challenges and high aspirations, Leatile King Baaitse and Asante Aupa Modala founded Oratory League to compete in the upcoming Orate Africa Championships – “Homecoming.”

Securing Independence

When traditional school sponsorships fell through, our founders persuaded the director to allow independent participation. Through tireless trips across Francistown, they secured sponsorships and registered 11 delegates with a modest total of P7,700.

Debate, Speech & Quiz Teams Unite

Our second delegation featured two debate teams – with Team A led by Baaitse, Modala, and Thabiso Morupisi-Khan, and Team B captained by Wangu Brandon Tsietso with Aone Mosimane and Phalana Lefhoko Tumiso – alongside a public speaking team and a quiz team that clinched 3rd runner-up honors.

First Representation & Triumph

The historic moment came when Leatile King Baaitse and Gracious Theresa Siolwe represented OL at the 2024 Cambridge School Debate – Africa Regional Round. They earned the title of best overall debate team in Africa and are set to represent the continent at Cambridge Union in 2025.

A Revolutionary Legacy

Today, Oratory League continues to grow – transforming education, pioneering an orate judiciary, and shaping a future where eloquence and critical thinking drive global change.
